Casting Machine Duration Improvement

This project focuses on increasing casting duration from minutes to over an hour through mechanical redesign, improved thermal interfaces, and vacuum optimization.

Problem Statement

The casting machine suffered from short casting duration due to thermal instability, uneven feed distribution, and vacuum leakage. These issues limited production efficiency and consistency.

Concepts

Several concepts were explored to improve thermal stability and feed uniformity:

  • Redesigning thermal interface plates
  • Modifying feed geometry for uniform distribution
  • Improving vacuum sealing and chamber integrity

Detailed Design

The detailed design phase included CAD modeling, FEA validation, and prototype testing. Key improvements:

  • New thermal interface plate with optimized heat transfer
  • Revised feed manifold geometry
  • Enhanced vacuum sealing using redesigned O-ring grooves

Final Assembly

The final assembly integrated all redesigned components, resulting in a casting duration increase from minutes to over an hour.
